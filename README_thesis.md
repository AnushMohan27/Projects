## Overview

Why do organizations invest heavily in analytics and AI — and still fail to act on the insights they produce?

This thesis investigates that gap. It examines how **consulting professionals translate analytics- and AI-driven insights into execution-level strategic decisions** within digital transformation initiatives, under real-world organizational constraints.

The central research question:

> *How do consulting professionals translate analytics- and AI-driven insights into execution-level strategic decisions within digital transformation initiatives under organizational constraints?*

---

## The Core Contribution: Analytics-to-Execution Translation Model (AETM)

The study develops and empirically validates the **Analytics-to-Execution Translation Model (AETM)** — a seven-stage mechanism describing how analytical outputs travel from generation to organizational action.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                     │
│  Stage 1 │ Insight Generation        │ Data scientists, analytics  │
│  Stage 2 │ Interpretive Mediation    │ Consultants, hybrid teams   │
│  Stage 3 │ Governance Mediation      │ Steering committees         │
│  Stage 4 │ Political-Cognitive Med.  │ Executives, stakeholders    │
│  Stage 5 │ Execution Decision        │ Business unit leaders       │
│  Stage 6 │ Operational Adaptation    │ Department teams            │
│  Stage 7 │ Implementation & Perf.    │ Operational leaders         │
│                                                                     │
│          ↑________________ Recursive feedback loop ________________ │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

The model is **not a rigid linear sequence**. It is an analytical map of the principal processes through which analytics insights are mediated, modified, and operationalized — or suppressed — on their path toward execution.

## Key Findings

### SRQ 1 — How consultants interpret and frame analytics insights
Four translation mechanisms identified:
- Framing as a strategic opportunity
- Staged, audience-calibrated communication
- Revenue-linked operationalization prioritization
- A practitioner-validated interpret–decide–execute–measure sequence

### SRQ 2 — Organizational constraints on execution
Four interacting constraint categories documented:
- Structural and governance constraints
- Resource and capability constraints
- Accountability and incentive constraints
- Cultural constraints

These combine to produce **patterned execution failure sequences**, most pervasively the **Budget-Lock-Drift sequence**.

### SRQ 3 — Governance architecture
**Hybrid hub-and-spoke governance models** dominate both observed configurations and practitioner recommendations. Governance structure conditions not only execution speed but the institutional authority of analytical recommendations.

### SRQ 4 — Political and cognitive mediation
Four micro-level processes identified through which analytics-driven recommendations are accepted, adapted, or suppressed:
- Leadership cognitive framing
- Informal power network dynamics
- Cultural selective adoption
- Alert fatigue

## Theoretical Contributions

1. **The AETM as an integrating framework** — a mechanism-centered model that extends and integrates the canonical literature on digital strategy, analytics capability, and strategy execution

2. **Consultant as translation agent** — theorizes the consulting professional as a distinctive intermediary whose interpretive, political, and communicative agency shapes whether analytics insights reach execution

3. **Execution drift reconceptualized** — reframes execution drift not as a failure or anomaly, but as a constitutive feature of effective translation in constrained organizational environments

4. **The recursive dependency problem** — identifies the condition in which the enablers required for effective translation are precisely the conditions that analytics-driven transformation is designed to produce

## What This Thesis Does Not Include

This is a **qualitative, theory-building study**. There is no quantitative dataset or code. The empirical base is eight expert interviews, analyzed using a structured Gioia coding process documented in the appendices.

The full thesis document is available on request. Interview data is anonymized (INT-01 through INT-08) in accordance with ethical guidelines agreed with participants.
